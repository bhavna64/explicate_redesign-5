Our Approach

Five Phases to
Proven AI Value

Discover
01

This phase focuses on defining the problem space — the science of knowing what to build.

  • Define Before You Derive — Articulate the problem, audience, and success criteria before any technical work begins.
  • Seek Isomorphisms — Use the Isomorphism Canvas to identify abstract problem structures and find analogous solutions.
  • Quantify the Outcome — Establish baseline metrics and business objectives to ensure ROI isn't optional.
Design
02

The system is modeled as a functional unit before AI is introduced.

  • Map the Domain — Understand the problem space as a system; AI amplifies chaos rather than resolving it.
  • Preserve the Human Invariant — Design the human-AI interaction model to ensure oversight remains constant.
  • Conjecture, Test, Prove — Define hypotheses and the kill criteria that determine when to pivot.
Build
03

Construction as an iterative process to earn trust and validate technical feasibility.

  • Earn Autonomy Incrementally — Start with "Assist" before moving to "Automate" so AI proves itself in steps.
  • Proof by Construction — Build the minimum system necessary to test the core hypothesis with real data.
Deploy
04

Transition from a controlled hypothesis to a functioning solution.

  • Validation of the Hypothesis — Use real-world evidence to support or refute the original conjecture.
  • Autonomy Boundaries — Define what the AI may do without human approval and set escalation rules for exceptions.
Support
05

Scaling the proven solution while maintaining the rigour of the system.

  • Continuous Convergence — Use iteration and feedback loops to ensure the system continues to meet performance goals as it scales.
  • Principled Exit — If a system fails its goals, use the Exit Protocol to capture learnings rather than continue investing.
